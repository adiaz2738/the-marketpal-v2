Diversification, Diversification, Diversification... Is it worth it?

Diversification is a protection against ignorance - Warren Buffett

Many look at diversification as THE strategy to make good returns in the stock market. Others view it as a good rule of thumb or some other odd thing. But the average strategies that most people recommend will get you just that. Average. You'll get nothing more than average returns with an average strategy. If you're diversifying your portfolio so much, you'd might as well just buy some index funds, because the returns you'll get on 20-50 mediocre stocks, is about the same or even far less than you'll obtain with 3-10 really good businesses. Remember you're not buying some digital thing that moves up and down on a chart, you're buying ownership in a company, and it seems like people have forgotten that over the years. So why should you treat it as anything lesser than that, a business you're buying? Diversification is only a good strategy if you're not very well educated on investing, because you won't be too vulnerable to the fluctuations of a stock in a specific industry. However, another thing to keep in mind is that when you're buying so many stocks, you're bound to buy some not so favorable ones. That would be businesses that have more debt than they can pay off, taking too many losses, it's poorly managed, dying business models, or even a high potential of bankruptcy, especially in a big bear market. When you're buying a bunch of companies that you don't understand, you're just holding on hope that one of those will turn out good, and the rest are a waste of money that could be put to work somewhere else.
